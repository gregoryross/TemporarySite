I model biomolecules at the atomistic scale

I am a postdoctoral research fellow at Memorial Sloan Kettering Cancer Center in the Choderal lab. I work on computational methods that help with the development of new medicines. Drug discovery is extremely costly and time consuming and my goal is to make this process cheaper and faster. My research is very interdisciplinary, and combines physics, biology, chemistry and statistics. In particular, I use computer simulations to predict which molecule from a set of candidates will be a better drug.
